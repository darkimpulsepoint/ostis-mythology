Zeus - in ancient Greek mythology, the god of the sky, thunder and lightning, in charge of the whole world. Chief of the Olympian gods, the third son of the god Kronos and the titanide Rhea; brother of Hades, Hestia, Demeter and Poseidon. Zeus's wife is the goddess Hera. In Roman mythology, he was identified with Jupiter. Zeus' attributes were: a shield and a double-sided ax (labrys), sometimes an eagle; the place of residence was considered to be Olympus (Zeus-Olympian). Zeus is thought of as "fire", "hot substance", dwelling in the ether, owning the sky, organizing the focus of cosmic and social life. In addition, Zeus distributes good and evil on earth, put shame and conscience in people.
