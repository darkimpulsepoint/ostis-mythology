Samaveda (Skt. सामवेद, IAST: sāmaveda, tatpurusha: सामन्, IAST: sāman, "ritual chant" and वेद, IAST: veda, "knowledge") is the third of the four Vedas, the ancient core of Hindu scriptures According to its holiness and significance for the ritual, "Samaveda", or "Veda of chants", comes immediately after the Rig Veda.
